Differences Between Japan and Canada

1. Social Etiquette

In Japan, politeness and consideration for others in public places are highly valued. For example, it is expected to be quiet on public transportation. In Canada, politeness is also important, but it is not as strict as in Japan, and casual conversations in public places are more common.

2. Food Culture

Japanese cuisine focuses on fresh seafood and seasonal ingredients, with an emphasis on presentation and balance. In Canada, the diverse immigrant population allows for a wide variety of international cuisines, with a focus on meat dishes and outdoor barbecues being particularly popular.

3. Interaction with Nature

Japan has distinct four seasons, each with traditional events and ways to enjoy nature. For example, cherry blossom viewing in spring is very popular. Canada is known for its vast natural landscapes, with winter sports like skiing and snowboarding being popular. Camping and hiking in large national parks are also common activities.
